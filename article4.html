Introducing Luke Chiang



Luke Chiang is fast becoming a prominent name in the indie-pop industry renowned for his emotional music that profoundly touches fans. Although he was born and raised in the USA, Luke’s career took off with his 2019 hit, “May I Ask?”, which demonstrated his knack at blending contemporary pop with R&B influences. His smooth, velvety voice combined with introspective lyrics have endeared him to his followers who find solace in storytelling.

The start of Luke’s path to music can be traced back to his college hostel room where he would post covers and original songs on YouTube. Within an instant, he began gaining recognition for songs that were sincere and full of raw emotion. As such, other songs like "Shouldn’t Be" and "Best Part" continued to build up his fan base especially amongst young people who could relate to themes of romance, vulnerability and self-discovery.


Chiang does minimalist production on most of his music so that there is only space left for his vocals. Most times you will hear light guitar melodies, subtle beats or harmonies layered perfectly creating a very soft almost meditative effect. This simplicity coupled with profound lyrics gives his work timeless appeal transcending various kinds of fan bases.

In addition to his solo work, Luke has also worked with other up and coming artists in the indie scene, adding to his fan base. Not only is he a remarkable artist for his ability to craft intricate emotions via words or melody in a packed music market but also one that writes very good songs. The continuous growth of his music database and the commitment of fans indicate that Luke Chiang will continue rising in the industry.


Luke Chiang remains an artist worth watching as he continues dropping new music since he brings a new, honest voice to the indie-pop genre. Regardless of whether you have been listening to him for ages or are just starting out, Luke’s music reflects on the highs and lows of existence; thereby positioning him as an influential figure among modern musicians.
